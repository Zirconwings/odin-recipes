Brick-Oven Pizza

Description:

This is a recipe for a simple pizza popularized by several Brooklyn wood-fire pizzarias. The pizza takes about 30 min to prep and 10 minutes to cook not including waiting time which is about 16 hours.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup cold water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6 ounces low moisture mozzarella chees thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup no salt added canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 6 leaves fresh basil, torn

Steps

  1. Make the dough: Pour warm water into a large bowl, then sprinkle yeast over the top. Let stand for 5 minutes. Stir in cold water and salt. Stir in flour, 1 cup at a time until incorporated
  2. Knead dough on a floured surface until smooth
  3. Divide dough in half, coat both in olive oil and refrigerate in a sealed container for at least 16 hours.
  4. Remove dough from refrigerator one hour before using it
  5. Dust the pizza stone with flour
  6. Preheat the overn to 550 degrees F with the pizza stone on the lowest rack
  7. Lightly dust one dough ball with flour and stretch until about the size of the pizza stone.
  8. Top pizza with mozzarella, oregano,tomatoes, and pepper
  9. Drizzle 1 tablespoon olive oil on top
  10. Bake pizza in oven until the crust begins to brown
  11. When crust is brown remove from oven and place to cool before serving
  12. When cooled cut and serve
  13. Repeat steps 5-12 for the second pizza
